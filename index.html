I started this venture because I've seen firsthand from my years of working in law firms what happens when a small law firm with one or two attorneys, or even a newly FL barred attorney, doesn't have the support to keep cases flowing and moving. Since 2020 I've been working in probate law firms learning the ins and outs of the craft of document preparation under two attorneys who I consider mentors, professionals who took the time to teach me everything I know and maintained and held my work to a high standard.

Over the years I joined other small law firms and the idea behind The Right Draft became crystal clear. Smaller firms and new attorneys face the same demands as large practices, but without the staff and resources to match. Hiring full time staff is expensive and uncertain. My firm is the alternative: an upfront, honest, and reliable document preparation professional at a fraction of the cost of staff, with none of the overhead.

My business model is simple. You are the first point of contact when a new client comes in, the face they see when they first meet to discuss probate and estate planning. You conduct the intake, gather the information from the client, and once the retainer is paid to your firm, I come in and immediately begin preparing petitions, orders, letters, affidavits, and motions for probate. For estate planning: the trust, will, power of attorney, and healthcare directives. Your next task is simply to follow up with the client to schedule a signing meeting, giving you time to review the documents and make any revisions before the client signs. Or you can now dedicate your time to expanding clientele, focusing on business strategy, and growing your practice and firm. Think of me as your dedicated legal document specialist, trading hours of paperwork preparation for hours back in your day. Less stress for all involved.

At some point after being mentored, my work became so consistent and excellent that the attorneys barely needed to review it before having the client sign and submitting to the clerk of court. That same level of consistent, excellent work is what I strive to bring to your practice.

Frequently asked questions

Everything Florida attorneys want to know before working with a freelance document preparer.

What is your turnaround time?

Standard turnaround is 2 to 3 business days for most documents. Complex formal administration matters with multiple filings may require 4 to 5 business days. If you have a court deadline or urgent need, mention it when you reach out, I will do my best to accommodate priority requests. Clear communication about timelines is a core part of how I work.

How do I send client information to you securely?

I accept intake documents and client information via secure file transfer methods including encrypted email, shared drives (Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive), or any secure portal your firm already uses. I do not retain client files beyond the scope of each engagement. If your firm has a specific transfer protocol, I am happy to work within it. Secure handling of confidential information is a non negotiable standard for every matter I work on.

What software and file formats do you work in?

I work in Microsoft Word (.docx) for all drafted documents, which makes it easy for your team to review, edit, and finalize before filing or execution. I can also deliver in PDF format upon request. If your firm uses specific templates or style guides, send them over and I will match your formatting exactly.

How many revisions are included?

Every engagement includes up to two rounds of revisions at no additional charge. My goal is to deliver work that requires minimal changes, my mentors regularly noted that my drafts needed little to no correction before submission. If a matter becomes significantly more complex than originally scoped, I will communicate that before any additional charges apply.

Is my client's information kept confidential?

Absolutely. All client information shared with The Right Draft is handled with the same confidentiality standard expected of any legal support professional. I do not share, sell, or disclose any client data to third parties. Files are used solely for the purpose of completing your requested documents and are not retained after delivery unless you request otherwise. I am happy to sign a confidentiality or non disclosure agreement if required by your firm.

Do you work with attorneys across all of Florida?

Yes. Document preparation services are available to attorneys and law firms anywhere in Florida, across all 67 counties. All drafting is completed remotely and delivered electronically, so geography is never a limitation. Whether you are in Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, Hillsborough, Orange, Duval, or any other Florida county, The Right Draft can assist your firm. Mobile notary and witnessing assistance is also available throughout Florida — feel free to inquire.

It's simple. Fill out the contact form below with your name, firm, and a brief description of your matter. I will respond within one business day to schedule a quick consultation, confirm the scope, and provide a timeline. From there, you send over the intake materials and I get to work. No retainer or long-term commitment required to start.
