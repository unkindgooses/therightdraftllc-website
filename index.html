About The Right Draft

Built from real legal experience — for the attorneys who need it most.

Hector A. Chavez Owner & Founder
The Right Draft

My name is Hector A. Chavez, and I started The Right Draft because I've seen firsthand what happens when a small firm or newly barred attorney doesn't have the support they need to keep cases moving. I've worked in probate law firms since 2020, learning the craft of Florida probate and estate planning documentation under two attorneys I consider mentors — professionals who took the time to train me and held my work to a high standard.

Over the years I joined other firms, and it was working at a smaller practice that the idea behind The Right Draft became clear. Smaller firms and new attorneys face the same document demands as large practices — but without the staff to match. Hiring full-time support is expensive and uncertain. I built The Right Draft to be the alternative: a reliable, on-call document preparation professional at a fraction of the cost of staff, with none of the overhead.

My model is simple. You handle client intake and consultation. I handle the drafting — petitions, inventories, wills, trusts, powers of attorney, healthcare directives, and everything in between — so you can focus on strategy, take on more clients, and grow your practice without getting buried in paperwork. Think of me as your dedicated legal document specialist, available when you need it, without the commitment of a full-time hire.

As the son of Spanish-speaking immigrants, I also understand firsthand the importance of accessible legal planning. I'm proud to offer document support and translation services for Spanish-speaking clients and firms serving Florida's Spanish-speaking communities.

My mentors told me they barely needed to review my work before submission. Now I want to bring that same quality to your practice.

Frequently asked questions

Everything attorneys want to know before getting started.

What is your turnaround time?

Standard turnaround is 2–3 business days for most documents. Complex formal administration matters with multiple filings may require 4–5 business days. If you have a court deadline or urgent need, mention it when you reach out — I will do my best to accommodate priority requests. Clear communication about timelines is a core part of how I work.

How do I send client information to you securely?

I accept intake documents and client information via secure file transfer methods including encrypted email, shared drives (Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive), or any secure portal your firm already uses. I do not retain client files beyond the scope of each engagement. If your firm has a specific transfer protocol, I am happy to work within it. Secure handling of confidential information is a non-negotiable standard for every matter I work on.

What software and file formats do you work in?

I work in Microsoft Word (.docx) for all drafted documents, which makes it easy for your team to review, edit, and finalize before filing or execution. I can also deliver in PDF format upon request. If your firm uses specific templates or style guides, send them over and I will match your formatting exactly.

How many revisions are included?

Every engagement includes up to two rounds of revisions at no additional charge. My goal is to deliver work that requires minimal changes — my mentors regularly noted that my drafts needed little to no correction before submission. If a matter becomes significantly more complex than originally scoped, I will communicate that before any additional charges apply.

Is my client's information kept confidential?

Absolutely. All client information shared with The Right Draft is handled with the same confidentiality standard expected of any legal support professional. I do not share, sell, or disclose any client data to third parties. Files are used solely for the purpose of completing your requested documents and are not retained after delivery unless you request otherwise. I am happy to sign a confidentiality or non-disclosure agreement if required by your firm.

Do you work with attorneys outside of Broward and Palm Beach Counties?

Yes — document preparation services are available to attorneys and law firms anywhere in Florida. All drafting is completed remotely and delivered electronically, so geography is not a limitation. Mobile notary and in-person witnessing services are currently available in Broward and Palm Beach Counties only.

How do I get started?

It's simple. Fill out the contact form below with your name, firm, and a brief description of your matter. I will respond within one business day to schedule a quick consultation, confirm the scope, and provide a timeline. From there, you send over the intake materials and I get to work. No retainer or long-term commitment required to start.
