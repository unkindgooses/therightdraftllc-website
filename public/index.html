About The Right Draft

An idea built from years of hands on law firm experience. So you get support and assistance that actually knows the work and more importantly, you get some peace of mind and some time back in your day.

I began this venture because I have seen firsthand from my time working in law firms what happens when a small firm with one to two attorneys and minimal staff, or even a newly Florida barred attorney, does not have the support or the time to put into drafting these critically important documents and keep cases flowing and moving. Therefore they fall behind and things slip through the cracks, which is never a good thing for attorneys and especially clients. Hence why The Right Draft was established to assist in these matters. Since 2020 I have been working in probate and estate planning law firms learning the ins and outs of this specific field of law and honing my skills under the guidance of two great attorneys who I personally consider my mentors. They took the time to teach me everything I know and always advised on how to make my work even better and held it to a high standard. That same high standard and quality of work is what I hope to bring to your firm.

Over the years I noticed small law firms and solo practitioners face the same demands and standards as large or medium sized law firms but without the proper support staff and resources to match. For smaller firms, hiring full time staff is expensive and uncertain. My firm offers a more affordable alternative: upfront, honest, and reliable document preparation at a fraction of the cost and with none of the headaches.

My business model is simple. You are the face and first point of contact when a new client retains your services to handle the complicated legal language and process that is probate and estate planning. You assess the client needs, note it on your intake form, and once they sign the retainer and pay your retainer fees, you come to us. Discuss your needs with us, we review the intake, you pay the retainer to use our service, and from there the intake is all I will need to prepare the documents required to complete the proceedings. Whether it is petitions, orders, letters, affidavits, and motions for probate, or the trust, will, power of attorney, and healthcare directives for estate planning, I will have it prepared. Once my task is complete and I have proofread the documents thoroughly, I will submit to you for final approval. If changes need to be made, that can be discussed for a small fee, but if it is just a name or address change that came up during the drafting process, those changes can be made fairly quickly. Once approved, my job is complete and you are free to schedule the signing appointment with the client as soon as possible, knowing the documents are ready and in your hands. From here I can work on your next case, or we say adieu for now. With document preparation out of your schedule you can now dedicate your time to expanding your clientele, focusing on business strategy, and growing your practice. Think of me as a dedicated legal assistant on speed dial, ready to trade those long and daunting hours of document preparation for hours back in your day and less stress overall.

As the son of Spanish speaking immigrants who may not be fully familiar with the legal process, I understand the importance of this work and how it protects families and their assets in the long term. I also know from experience that our Spanish speaking community may be hesitant to pursue this paperwork, but knows it is a necessary step in life. I am happy to offer translation assistance to clients upon request, which could open up a whole new client base you may not have been able to reach otherwise.

After some time being mentored I was able to branch out on my own and deliver high quality and consistent work that the attorneys I worked for no longer needed to review the entire packet of documents I presented to them. They needed one quick glance and knew my work was up to their high standard. That same consistency, quality of work, high standard, and trust is what I hope to bring to you as well.

How it works

A clear process that supports your legal workflow.

Consultation

We discuss your needs and how The Right Draft can assist you with those needs and you choose the service(s) you need. This is where you would bring to my attention if a file needs to be expedited or if the normal turnaround time will suffice. We also discuss any potential additional assistance I might be able to provide for you, as well as answer any additional questions you may have.

Finalize the Service Agreement and Sign

At this point all questions have been answered and you are ready to move forward with my service. I send you a copy of the service agreement that will be digitally signed and payment will be due at the time of signing said agreement. Work cannot begin until the agreement is signed and payment is received in full.

Drafting Documents

I begin drafting the documents you require based on the service you chose. Documents will be prepared in accordance with Florida Statutes and Code and with any additional instructions from the attorney.

Proofread & Deliver

I will do one final diligent review of documents before I confirm with the attorney what safe avenue they wish to use for delivery. Once delivered, the attorney will conduct one final review and confirm if my task is complete. Once I receive the green light my work is complete with said attorney until they require my services again.

Frequently asked questions

Everything Florida attorneys want to know before working with a freelance document preparer.

What is your turnaround time?

Standard turnaround is 3 to 5 business days for most document packages. Rush files can be accommodated with proper notice and payment of the rush fee. Same day rush files can also be accommodated with proper notice. I will always do my best to accommodate rush file requests. If for some reason I cannot fulfill your request, I will be upfront and let you know immediately. Clear and direct communication is one of the core values of how I work.

How do I send client information to you securely?

I accept intake documents and client information via secure file transfer methods including encrypted email, shared drives (Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive), or any secure portal your firm already uses. I do not retain client files beyond the scope of each engagement. If your firm has a specific transfer protocol, I am happy to work within it. Secure handling of confidential information is a nonnegotiable standard for every matter I work on.

What file formats do I work in?

I work in Microsoft Word (.docx) for all drafted documents, which makes it easy for your team to review, edit, and finalize before filing or execution. I can also deliver in PDF format upon request. If your firm uses specific templates or style guides, send them over and I will match your formatting exactly.

How many revisions are included?

Every engagement includes one round of revisions. If I make an error or a name needs to be changed after the initial documents were completed, I can include a second round of changes at no extra charge. My goal is to deliver quality work correctly on the first attempt. If a matter suddenly becomes significantly more complex than originally scoped, we will discuss on a quick call how to proceed.

Is my client's information kept confidential?

100% absolutely. Just like there is attorney client confidentiality, I maintain a confidentiality agreement with the hiring attorney or law firm to handle sensitive information with the utmost care, respect, and privacy. I will NOT share, sell, or disclose any client information to any third party. Client information is used solely for the purpose of completing the paperwork required by the attorney to complete legal proceedings. Once my task with the attorney is complete, the sensitive information is safely discarded and never saved in any The Right Draft files or records. If the hiring firm or attorney has an NDA or confidentiality agreement, I will be the first to sign it to ensure a strong level of trust and peace of mind. That is another core value of how I work.

Do you work with attorneys across all of Florida?

Yes. Document preparation services are available to attorneys and law firms anywhere in Florida, across all 67 counties. All drafting is completed remotely and delivered electronically, so geography is never a limitation. Whether you are in Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, Hillsborough, Orange, Duval, or any other Florida county, The Right Draft can assist your firm. Mobile notary and witnessing assistance is also available throughout Florida, feel free to inquire.

Why choose The Right Draft instead of hiring staff?

With The Right Draft, you do not need to worry about salary (unless you have me on a monthly retainer), benefits, or training costs that come with hiring staff. Retaining staff is just as difficult and stressful. Pay per matter only, and it scales with your caseload whether it is a busy season or a slow one. Think of me as a legal assistant on speed dial, ready to work. With us, you know exactly where your money is going, and it is going toward high quality work and consistent service.
